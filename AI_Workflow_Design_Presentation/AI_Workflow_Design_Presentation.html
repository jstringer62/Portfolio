Why This Works

Three Core Principles

1 SME Intake Optimization
Interviews guided by AI let any trained interviewer capture expert quality content. Senior staff review outputs instead of attending every session. SMEs provide expertise; AI handles structure and gaps.
2 AI-Enabled Storyboarding
Initial storyboards within strict guardrails: template enforcement, objective anchoring, confidence flagging. Every output traces to SME input. No hallucination, nothing without purpose.
3 Adult Learning Alignment
Grounded in andragogy: Need to Know, Problem Centered, Experience Based. AI accelerates execution, not decisions. Full designer override authority.
The Foundation

The quality of SME intake sets the ceiling for everything downstream. AI cannot rescue poorly captured source material. It can only accelerate well-structured inputs. This makes intake the highest-leverage point for human involvement.

The Problem This Solves

Senior staff often attend SME meetings because junior team members lack expertise to know what questions to ask. This workflow enables any trained interviewer to conduct quality intake, with Directors reviewing outputs rather than attending every session. Once proven, portions of the workflow can be further automated.

What Gets Captured

SMEs provide what AI can't: misconceptions, edge cases, what mastery looks like.
AI handles the rest: sequencing, structure, gap detection.
A Host Guide walks any interviewer through questions adapted to the domain and tied to objectives.

Phase 1

Initiation & Scoping

Before any SME time is scheduled, scope must be defined, validated, and approved. This phase ensures everyone is aligned on what we're building and how success will be measured.

1 Leadership Kickoff Human
Leadership provides the starting inputs: goals, topics, target audience, course parameters, and any known constraints. Critically, this must include how knowledge transfer will be measured: post-course assessments, certifications, on-the-job metrics? These requirements shape assessment design throughout.
2 Outline Generation AI
AI agent builds an initial course outline from leadership's input: module structure, high-level objectives, and target outcomes.
Domain adaptation: Outline structure adapts to the domain. Accounting courses need regulatory constraints and calculation logic; clinical courses need patient scenarios and decision trees.
3 L&D Validation AI
L&D Agent validates learning design methodology and defines where assessments occur in the structure, linked to objectives. This shapes interview questions (what does mastery look like for each objective?).
Confidence flagging: Low-confidence sections marked for human review before proceeding.
4 Leadership Approval Human
Leadership approves the validated outline as the starting point. Depending on training size, this outline may be broken down further before proceeding to interview preparation.
Why This Matters

This phase defines scope and assessment strategy before SME time is scheduled. The interviewer enters knowing what objectives to probe and what mastery should look like, not open-ended exploration.

Phase 2

Interview Preparation

AI prepares the interview framework so any trained interviewer can conduct a high-quality SME session, freeing Directors from routine meetings.

5 Question Generation AI
Interview Expert Agent crafts questions to maximize topic coverage and address approved goals from Phase 1.
Question types: Conceptual (what/why), procedural (how), edge cases (what if), and mastery definition (what does good look like).
6 Host Guide Preparation AI
Questions structured into a guided interview flow. The Host Guide walks the interviewer through each question, prompts follow-ups, and captures responses for downstream use.
Format options: Static document (ensures all questions answered) or live AI interface with step-by-step pages (more flexible, real-time guidance).
The Value of AI-Guided Interviews

This approach enables any trained interviewer to conduct expert-level SME sessions. The AI knows what questions to ask; the human builds rapport and captures nuance. Directors review outputs rather than attending every meeting.

A Note on Automation

While further automation is possible (AI conducting interviews directly, fully automated question flows), the recommendation is to start with humans in the loop and document the nuances that emerge. Once the process is refined and edge cases are well-understood, selective automation can be explored, but human judgment in live SME interactions captures subtleties that pure automation may miss.

Phase 3

SME Intake & Synthesis

An iterative intake loop: interview, synthesize, identify gaps, follow up with SME, then combine and validate. This ensures comprehensive capture before storyboarding.

The Iterative Loop

A single pass rarely captures everything. This phase builds in a structured feedback loop: Interview → Synthesize → Reflect on gaps → Generate follow-up questions → Address gaps (same session or scheduled follow-up) → Combine → Validate. The goal is to surface and address gaps early, not discover them during storyboarding.

7 SME Interview (Initial) Human + AI SME
The interviewer conducts the initial SME session with AI guidance. The Host Guide prompts each question, captures responses in real-time, and flags unexplored areas.
Recording: Tools like Otter.ai, Fireflies.ai, Read AI, or MeetGeek provide live transcripts that feed directly into synthesis.
8 Initial Synthesis AI
AI synthesizes captured information and structures it to surface gaps: missing topics, incomplete explanations, areas that need deeper probing.
9 L&D Adaptation AI
L&D Agent restructures content for proper methodology and flags where principles need strengthening. Also checks if SME revealed new assessment opportunities not in the original structure.
Confidence flagging: Low-confidence sections marked for human review.
10 Gap Reflection AI
A Reflection Agent compiles gaps from both the Synthesis Agent (content gaps) and the L&D Agent (methodology gaps) into a consolidated gap report.
11 Follow-up Q Generation AI
Interview Expert Agent generates targeted follow-up questions to fill identified gaps. The interviewer reviews and selects which questions to use. This is the approval step.
12 Follow-up Questions Human + AI SME
Targeted follow-up to address identified gaps. This could be a separate session scheduled later, or a continuation of the same meeting after a brief break while AI processes and generates follow-up questions. Flexibility depends on SME availability and gap complexity.
13 Combined Synthesis AI
AI merges both interview data sets into a unified, comprehensive synthesis, filling gaps from Session 1 with content from Session 2.
14 Final L&D Validation AI
L&D Agent performs final validation on combined synthesis. Confirms learning design standards met and ready for human review.
Confidence flagging: Low-confidence sections highlighted for Learning Scientist attention.
15 L&D Scientist Review Human
Learning Scientist reviews the text-based synthesis: objectives scoped for time, all content tied to objectives (nothing floating without purpose). If issues found, they edit directly. Approved version moves to storyboarding.
Future enhancement: AI-assisted editing tools could speed up revisions at this stage.
Must Come From SMEs
  • Common misconceptions learners have
  • Edge cases and exceptions to rules
  • Real-world scenarios and examples
  • Domain-specific constraints (regulatory, technical)
  • What mastery actually looks like
  • Why things are done a certain way
AI Can Infer or Generate
  • What order to teach things (teach A before B)
  • What learners need to know before starting
  • Linking related concepts together
  • Draft quiz questions (humans validate accuracy)
  • Formatting content into templates
  • Spotting what's missing or incomplete
Input Sources

Leadership (Phase 1) defines objectives, constraints, and how success is measured. SMEs provide the expertise: misconceptions, edge cases, real-world scenarios. AI assists with structuring, sequencing, and drafting supplementary content.

Phase 4

Storyboard Development

AI generates first-pass storyboards within strict guardrails. Humans validate instructional integrity and alignment to adult learning principles.

16 Structure Generation AI
AI builds the storyboard skeleton from validated synthesis: module flow, section breaks, content placement. Follows approved templates exactly.
17 L&D Validation AI
L&D Agent validates structure against learning design methodologies: scaffolding, cognitive load, assessment alignment. Flags issues before draft generation.
Confidence flagging: Low-confidence sections marked for Learning Scientist review.
18 Draft Generation AI
AI populates the structure with first-pass content: explanations, examples, assessment items. Every block traces to an objective; gaps flagged, not filled with fluff.
19 L&D Scientist Review Human
Learning Scientist reviews the draft for tone, cognitive load, instructional flow. Rewrites where needed. Full override authority. AI suggestions are starting points, not decisions.
20 SME + L&D Review SME Human
SME validates technical accuracy; Learning Scientist ensures instructional flow. Can happen together or separately. If SME corrections affect structure, iterate until both approve.
Guardrails Against Low-Quality Outputs
  • Template enforcement: AI outputs conform to approved structure; no freeform generation
  • Objective anchoring: Every content block traces to an objective; unlinked content flagged
  • Confidence thresholds: Low-confidence sections marked for mandatory human review
  • No silent failures: AI surfaces gaps rather than generating placeholder content
Adult Learning Alignment
  • Need to Know: Workflow captures why before what. Relevance designed in, not retrofitted
  • Experience: SME scenarios connect new content to real-world context learners recognize
  • Self-Direction: Clear objectives let learners track their own progress
  • Problem-Centered: Content organized around tasks and decisions, not abstract topics
  • Assessment Integration: Measurement requirements set in Phase 1; SMEs define mastery; every assessment maps to an objective
